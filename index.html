Safe RL based System for Connected and Autonomous Vehicle Charging Infrastructure

Undergraduate Thesis

Thesis Score: 97/100

This undergraduate thesis focused on designing an end-to-end intelligent system that provides optimal charging recommendations for connected and autonomous vehicles. Leveraging multi-agent system, the research incorporated Safe Reinforcement Learning to ensure decision-making within predefined safety constraints, combined with deep neural networks and multiple linear models to improve prediction accuracy. The system demonstrated significant improvements over baseline approaches, particularly in managing crowded charging requests while maintaining efficiency and safety.

Safe Reinforcement Learning Multi-Agent Systems Deep Neural Networks Autonomous Vehicles
Jan 2023 – Jan 2024

Evaluating Online Sexism Detection

Published Research - IEEE I2CT 2024

This research explored Natural Language Processing techniques to detect online sexism using the EDOS dataset. Implemented in Python with NLTK and Keras, the study conducted a comparative analysis of various machine learning models, evaluating their effectiveness in identifying sexist language across different contexts. The findings contributed to understanding model strengths and weaknesses in handling nuanced online discourse, and the work was published in the 2024 IEEE 9th International Conference for Convergence in Technology (I2CT).
